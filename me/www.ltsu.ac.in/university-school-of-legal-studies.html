About University School of Legal studies

The University School of Legal Studies (USLS) at Lamrin Tech Skills University, Punjab, has been established with the aims to nurture competent, ethical, and socially responsible legal professionals who can meet the challenges of a rapidly changing global legal environment. With a strong emphasis on research, innovation, and skill-based learning, USLS is committed to developing critical thinkers, advocates, policymakers, and academicians. The School integrates modern pedagogy with practical exposure through moot courts, internships, legal aid clinics, and collaborations with the judiciary, law firms, corporate sector, and policy-making institutions.
USLS aspires to emerge as a premier center for legal education in the region, contributing to the advancement of justice, legal reforms, and rule of law, while fostering values of equity, fairness, and professional integrity among its students.

Establishment of the Centre

In compliance with the UGC Guidelines for the introduction of one year LL.M. Degree Program, the Centre of Post-Graduate Legal Studies (CPGLS) of University School of Legal Studies, Lamrin Tech Skills University was established on 20-09-2025. With the establishment of the Centre the University initiated LL.M. One Year Degree Course from the Academic Year 2025-26.

Vision

To emerge as a center of excellence in advanced legal education and research, nurturing globally competent legal scholars and practitioners committed to justice, equity, and innovation in the evolving landscape of law.


Mission

  • To provide rigorous and specialized postgraduate legal education that integrates theoretical knowledge with practical application.

  • To foster a culture of critical thinking, ethical responsibility, and interdisciplinary research in the field of law.

  • To equip students with advanced legal skills, analytical capabilities, and comparative perspectives to address contemporary challenges in law and governance.

  • To build strong academia–industry–judiciary linkages that enhance professional opportunities and policy engagement.

  • To prepare graduates as leaders, educators, policymakers, and advocates who contribute meaningfully to the justice system and society at large.

LL.M One Year Postgraduate Programme

The duration of the course shall be one academic year, divided into two semesters and Consisting of 36 weeks i.e. semesters First and second (Of 18 weeks each). The course shall be for whole time students. There shall be 10 papers (5 Papers in First semester & 4 Papers and one dissertation in second semester)

The examination for the 1st semester shall ordinarily be held in the month of December/January and for the 2nd semester in the month of May/June on such dates as may be notified by the Controller of Examinations of the LTSU Punjab

A supplementary examination for the 1st semester shall be held along with the IInd semester. However, the supplementary examination for the IInd semester shall be held after one month of the declaration of result.


ADMISSION PROCEDURE

  • Admission to one year- LL.M. Program shall be done through on merit based on the recommendation of admission committee headed by Dean Law.

  • The University has a right to keep the seats vacant if there are no candidates found suitable. Candidates awaiting results of the qualifying examination cannot be allowed to be admitted in the LL.M one-year program however the candidates will be permitted with the prior approval of competent authority.

  • The total number of seats will be 120 i.e. (60+60). The rules of reservation of the Punjab Government will be applicable accordingly.

  • 25% seats over and above of the sanctioned seats are reserved for Foreign National Category and allotted to International students. These seats are exclusively for students holding foreign passports and having a valid visa.

  • The vacant seats under Foreign National Category shall not be filled with other then international students.


The detailed eligibility conditions e.g. percentage of marks in the qualifying examinations, subject(s) combination etc. shall be notified in the Admission Brochure, as approved by the Academic Council from time to time. Candidates placed under compartment in the qualifying examination shall not be eligible for admission

Eligibility for Admission

  • To be eligible to appear for admission to One year- LL.M. Programme, a candidate should have passed the LL.B or an equivalent degree from a recognised University with not less than 50% marks in aggregate (45% in case of reserved categories, i.e. SC/ST/Persons with Disability).

  • The admission for Foreign Nationals shall be made on the basis of:
    (i) academic record of LL.B. or equivalent Degree recognized by UGC/ Association of Indian Universities with not less than 50% marks or equivalent grade.
    (ii) assessment of necessary communication skills in written and spoken English
